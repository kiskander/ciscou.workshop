üìö Workshop Content & Prerequisites

What are the prerequisites for this workshop?

+

Basic networking knowledge is recommended but not required. Familiarity with IP addressing, basic routing concepts, and command-line interfaces will be helpful. We provide foundational materials for beginners.

What topics will be covered in the workshop?

+

The workshop covers Cisco networking fundamentals, advanced routing and switching, network security, automation, and best practices for instruction. Check our detailed agenda for the complete schedule.

Will I receive a certificate of completion?

+

Yes! All participants who complete the workshop will receive a digital certificate of completion from Cisco U. and NetAcad. Certificates will be emailed within 48 hours after the workshop concludes.

üíª Technical Requirements

What equipment do I need for the workshop?

+

You'll need a laptop or desktop computer with a stable internet connection. We recommend having Cisco Packet Tracer installed (free download from NetAcad). All other tools and simulators will be provided or accessible online.

Do I need to install any specific software?

+

Cisco Packet Tracer is recommended for hands-on labs. You'll also need a PDF reader for documentation and a modern web browser for accessing online resources. All software requirements are listed in the pre-workshop email.

What are the minimum system requirements?

+

Windows 10/11, macOS 10.14+, or Linux Ubuntu 18.04+. Minimum 4GB RAM (8GB recommended), 2GB free disk space, and broadband internet connection. Mobile devices can access materials but aren't suitable for hands-on labs.

üìÖ Registration & Attendance

How do I register for the workshop?

+

Registration is typically handled through your local Cisco partner or NetAcad institution. Contact your regional coordinator or check the Cisco U. portal for registration links and availability.

Is attendance mandatory for all sessions?

+

While we encourage full attendance for the best learning experience, we understand scheduling conflicts may arise. All sessions are recorded and materials remain accessible. However, certificate eligibility may require minimum attendance.

Can I access materials after the workshop ends?

+

Yes! All workshop materials remain available on this site for 6 months after the workshop. You can download resources, access recordings, and continue using the collaborative tools during this period.

Can I use these materials in my own classes?

+

Absolutely! All materials are provided under Creative Commons licensing for educational use. You can adapt, modify, and use them in your own courses. Please maintain attribution to Cisco U. and NetAcad.

How do I access the Miro collaboration board?

+

The Miro board link is provided in the Resources section. You'll need to create a free Miro account if you don't have one. The board remains active throughout the workshop and for 30 days afterward.

Are there mobile-friendly versions of the materials?

+

This website is fully responsive and works on mobile devices. However, for the best experience with hands-on labs and interactive content, we recommend using a laptop or desktop computer.
